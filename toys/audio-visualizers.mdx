# Audio Visualizers

Audio visualizers transform sound into immersive visual experiences. These toys analyze frequency bands, track beat intensity, and respond to audio dynamics in real-time.

<Info>
  All audio visualizers support both **microphone input** and **demo audio** fallback for seamless exploration.
</Info>

## Audio Visualizer Features

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Frequency Analysis">
    Audio visualizers analyze frequency bands to drive different visual elements:
    
    - **Bass (low frequencies)**: Drive large-scale motion and beat pulses
    - **Mids (vocal range)**: Control color shifts and mid-level animation
    - **Highs (treble)**: Trigger sparkle, shimmer, and fine detail
    
    Toys like **Synesthetic Visualizer** and **Microphone Geometry Visualizer** expose frequency mapping controls for fine-tuning.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Beat Detection">
    Many visualizers include **beat detection** that tracks audio peaks and triggers visual bursts:
    
    - **Spiral Burst**: Expands spirals on beat peaks
    - **Grid Visualizer**: Bounces cubes/spheres with beat intensity
    - **Bubble Harmonics**: Inflates bubbles on audio peaks
    
    Look for **Beat sensitivity** or **Reactivity** controls to adjust detection thresholds.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Shader-Based Effects">
    Several visualizers leverage custom shaders for real-time effects:
    
    - **Neon Wave**: Four color themes with bloom effects
    - **Audio Light Show**: Shader style switching and palette shuffle
    - **MilkDrop Proto**: Feedback engine with warp effects
    
    These toys often include **Quality presets** to balance visual fidelity with performance.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Getting Started with Audio Visualizers

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose Your Audio Source">
    Select between **microphone input** for live audio reactivity or **demo audio** for pre-mixed tracks.
    
    <Tip>
      Start with demo audio to explore without microphone permission prompts.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Try a Starter Preset">
    Featured visualizers include starter presets optimized for first-time users. Look for the preset button in the control panel.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Adjust Sensitivity">
    Use **Sensitivity**, **Reactivity**, or **Beat response** controls to fine-tune how aggressively the toy responds to audio.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Explore Quality Settings">
    If performance is sluggish, try the **Quality presets** in the settings panel to reduce rendering load.
  </Step>
</Steps>
